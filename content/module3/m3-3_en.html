Cultural competency

Developing Cultural competency is an ongoing process. As a public service employee, you are invited to:

  • increase your cultural knowledge
  • raise your cultural awareness
  • develop your skills to create a culturally safe environment

Knowledge requires learning about our cultural diversity, our colonial history, and the current health and socio-economic status of Indigenous peoples in Canada.

Awareness involves recognizing your assumptions and beliefs about Indigenous peoples and how they affect your relationships.

Skills are the ways you create effective and respectful interactions with Indigenous peoples.

In other words, cultural competency helps you:

  • communicate with people of another culture in a way that earns respect and trust
  • build cooperative and productive partnerships at work
  • adapt your professional skills to different community perspectives

Definition : Cultural competency

Cultural competency is being able to use skills to develop effective relationships by applying what you know about yourself and others. It is an ongoing or developmental journey.
