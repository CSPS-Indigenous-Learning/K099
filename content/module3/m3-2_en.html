Cultural sensitivity

As a public service employee, you work with policies, directives and legislation. You also need to be sensitive to human realities. Like everyone else, Indigenous people want to be included, welcomed, acknowledged and respected.

Cultural sensitivity is about being able to accept and embrace differences. It is also about considering those differences as strengths.

Take time to participate in Indigenous cultural events in your region. Consider visiting a Friendship Centre! Be open and ask questions.

Definition : Cultural sensitivity

Cultural sensitivity is being aware of how one's own experiences and values may impact others. It also means recognizing that different experiences are valid.
