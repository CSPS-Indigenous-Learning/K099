Communication and collaboration

To build culturally safe relationships with Indigenous peoples, try following these 5 important steps:

  1. Listen with empathy to understand the challenges
    • Take your time and do not rush
    • Be curious and ensure you understand by asking questions
  2. Explain your perspective and limitations while taking into consideration the challenges
    • Be honest and transparent about constraints and requirements
    • Be sensitive
  3. Acknowledge and discuss the gaps
    • Identify and explore differences respectfully
    • Identify shared perspectives, goals and desires
  4. Discuss options and make recommendations
    • Consider all options and try to think outside the box
    • Work together on an action plan that values all perspectives
  5. Negotiate and arrive at an acceptable agreement
    • Ensure there is mutual agreement that is understood by all

Building respectful relationships takes time and energy. As public service employees, we often need to get things done quickly. This can have consequences. Building trust and respect with Indigenous peoples, partners and communities will take us less time in the long run—and we’ll have better results.

Using these 5 steps can start a working relationship off on the right foot. These steps will help you keep the relationship strong and collaborative.

People want to feel respected. More often than not, they are open to sharing what that means to them.
