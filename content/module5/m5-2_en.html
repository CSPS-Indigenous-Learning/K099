Building stronger and safer relationships

Learning about others and understanding their viewpoints will help you build respectful, stronger and safer relationships.

We all tend to be more collaborative and approachable when we feel respected and understood. But keep in mind that respect means different things for different people.

Self-reflection: What does respect look like for you?

When you work with individuals or a community you are not familiar with, consider learning about their “cultural protocols.” These are their ways of showing respect.

Did you know?

There is a view in mainstream Canadian culture that people who do not maintain eye contact are insincere or disinterested. However, some cultures do not favour direct eye contact. In some cases, direct eye contact is seen as intimidating or disrespectful.

Some Indigenous people will not maintain eye contact. They may look over your shoulder or to the side of you. For those who attended residential schools, direct eye contact with church officials would often result in physical punishment.
