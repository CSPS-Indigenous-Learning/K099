Discrimination

According to the Canadian Human Rights Commission, discrimination is an action or a decision that treats a person or a group badly for reasons such as race, colour, nationality or ethnic origin, and genetic characteristics. These reasons are protected under the Canadian Human Rights Act. The Act also protects people from discrimination in specific contexts, such as employment, housing and obtaining services.

Discrimination puts stereotypes and biases into action negatively in a way that directly impacts people. Sometimes you may not even realize that discrimination has happened.

How do you know whether you have discriminated against an Indigenous person? You can examine the incident to see if:

  • the Indigenous person was treated negatively
  • being Indigenous was a contributing factor in the treatment

Discrimination may feel out of your control. However, behaviours are within your control. Behaviour is a place where you can begin to take action.

Self-reflection: Identify an aspect of your work that might have a negative impact on Indigenous peoples. Considering what you have learned, could this example be considered discrimination? Why or why not?
