Unconscious bias

Unconscious bias refers to thoughts and feelings that occur outside of our awareness. They are learned stereotypes that are unintentional and deeply engrained. Unconscious biases can influence behaviours. Together they can lead to an unfair treatment of a group of people.

Self-reflection: When you think about an “Indigenous person,” what characteristics come to mind? Where do you think these characteristics come from? We all hold biases without realizing it. Reflection is part of learning about our biases.

You may hear people say, “I feel the same way towards all people, regardless of how they look or the colour of their skin.” They may also say, “I treat everyone the same—race is irrelevant.” These kinds of statements suggest this person believes they have no racial bias.

Part of developing cultural competency is to be aware of your biases. That way you can use tools and resources to help adjust your patterns of thinking and end any discriminatory behaviours. It’s a long journey.

When YouTube launched their video upload app for iOS, some users had videos showing upside down. Do you know why? The app was designed for right-handed users. The workers were most likely right-handed people and didn’t consider the business impacts on the left-handed.
