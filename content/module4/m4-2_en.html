Prejudice and unconscious biases

When we accept stereotypes as facts, we are generalizing and may be developing attitudes of prejudice. It is important to be aware of this process. The question to ask ourselves is not whether the stereotype is true or false. The question is, are we overgeneralizing?

Consider the questions below:

Do you avoid people from a specific racial group? Do you prefer to socialize with people from your own racial group?

It is not surprising if you answered yes to either of these questions. Preference for members of one’s own social group is common. While it may be uncomfortable, it’s important to be aware of this bias.

Self-reflection: Think about a particular person or situation and ask yourself these questions:

  • What are my biases?
  • Are my biases automatic thoughts?
  • How do they make me feel?
  • What will help me understand my biases?

Having a bias towards something or someone means having a preference for —or a prejudice against—one thing, person or group compared with another. This does not automatically mean discrimination.
