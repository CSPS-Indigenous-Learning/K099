Race matters

Race refers to a group of people of common ancestry, who are socially defined as being different from other groups. Race is implied in images, stereotypes, prejudices and discrimination. All of this has a direct impact on the lives of Indigenous peoples.

It may seem reasonable to view everyone as the same; we are all members of the human race. However, if you ignore race you may not see the power inequities and privileges of our society that are based in race. If we are to create culturally safe relationships with Indigenous peoples, we need knowledge about the impact of race, and we need to critically assess our ideas about race and racism.

Ethnicity

Ethnicity is made up of the many beliefs, behaviours and traditions that hold a group together. These include a common language, history, geographical location and religion. Ethnic diversity is the presence of various ethnic groups within a society or nation.

Ethnic groups are often non-dominant.
