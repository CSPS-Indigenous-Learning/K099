Taking action to understand the truth

Speech from the Throne

On December 4, 2015, His Excellency the Right Honourable David Johnston, Governor General of Canada, stated in his Speech from the Throne:

Because it is both the right thing to do and a certain path to economic growth, the Government will undertake to renew, nation-to-nation, the relationship between Canada and Indigenous peoples, one based on recognition of rights, respect, co-operation and partnership.

The Truth and Reconciliation Commission’s Report

Also in 2015, the Truth and Reconciliation Commission of Canada released its report with 94 Calls to Action. They aim to “redress the legacy of residential schools and advance the process of Canadian reconciliation.”

Call to Action #57 asks federal, provincial, territorial and municipal governments to provide education to public service employees on:

  • the history of Indigenous peoples, including the legacy of residential schools
  • the United Nations Declaration on the Rights of Indigenous Peoples
  • Treaties and Aboriginal rights
  • Indigenous law
  • Aboriginal-Crown relations

We designed this course for federal public service employees in answer to Call to Action #57. Our goal is to raise awareness and increase knowledge about the shared history between Canada and Indigenous peoples. This training will serve to develop skills and enhance cultural competence.

It’s crucial that public service employees take action towards reconciliation for our country.
