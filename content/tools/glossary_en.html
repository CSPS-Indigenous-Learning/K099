Glossary

  • Culture

    Culture can be defined as the shared values, norms, family styles, social roles and behaviours of a group of people. The people may have developed these common traits in response to the political, economic, environmental and social realities they face. Culture also refers to the things they share based on common ethnicity, race and/or nationhood.

  • Race

    Race refers to a group of people of common ancestry, who are socially defined as being different from other groups.

  • Ethnicity

    Ethnicity is made up of the many beliefs, behaviours and traditions that hold a group together. These include a common language, history, geographical location and religion. Ethnic diversity is the presence of various ethnic groups within a society or nation.

  • Cultural awareness

    Cultural awareness is understanding the cultural differences between groups, while not assigning a value to those differences.

  • Cultural sensitivity

    Cultural sensitivity is being aware of how one's own experiences and values may impact others. It also means recognizing that different experiences are valid.

  • Cultural competency

    Cultural competency is being able to use skills to develop effective relationships by applying what you know about yourself and others. It is an ongoing or developmental journey.

  • Cultural humility

    Cultural humility is being able to see your own culture in relation to other cultures without a sense of superiority

  • Cultural safety

    Cultural safety focuses on responsive services. Its goal is to create equitable health and socio-economic outcomes for Indigenous people.
